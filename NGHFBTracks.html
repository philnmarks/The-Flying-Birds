Release Notes

Released on October 17, 2011, Noel Gallagher’s High Flying Birds marked Noel Gallagher’s bold first step into his solo career after the dissolution of Oasis. The self-titled debut album blends Noel’s classic songwriting with fresh, unexpected influences, ranging from rock and orchestral sounds to psychedelic undertones. Produced by Noel Gallagher alongside Dave Sardy, the album features standout tracks like “The Death of You and Me”, “If I Had a Gun...”, and the rhythmic, anthemic “AKA... What a Life!”. Gallagher’s distinctive voice and melodic hooks are ever-present, but the album also reveals a more adventurous and experimental side, weaving in brass sections, lush strings, and groove-driven beats. Key Collaborators: Jeremy Stacey (drums) Russ Pritchard (bass) Mike Rowe (keyboards) Becky Byrne (trumpet) Crouch End Festival Chorus (backing vocals) The album achieved critical and commercial success, reaching #1 on the UK Albums Chart and receiving widespread acclaim for its fresh yet familiar sound. Noel Gallagher’s High Flying Birds introduced a new chapter in Noel’s musical journey, combining the best of his past with a forward-thinking approach, setting the stage for the continued success of his solo career.

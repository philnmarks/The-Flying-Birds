Noel Gallagher's High Flying Birds

Noel Gallagher's High Flying Birds have released three distinct EPs, each marking a creative evolution for the band and Noel Gallagher's experimentation as a solo artist. "Black Star Dancing" (2019): This EP saw Gallagher moving into new musical territories, blending his classic rock style with elements of funk, disco, and electronic music. The title track, "Black Star Dancing," is a danceable, groove-laden tune inspired by artists like David Bowie and Nile Rodgers. The EP also includes tracks like "Rattling Rose" and "Sail On," which combine a mix of pop and psychedelic influences, showcasing Noel’s willingness to experiment with his sound while retaining his melodic instincts. "This Is The Place" (2019): Released a few months after Black Star Dancing, this EP continues Noel's exploration of more eclectic sounds. The title track, "This Is The Place," is a cosmic, psychedelic anthem with swirling instrumentation and expansive production. The EP features the track "A Dream Is All I Need to Get By," which channels 60s pop influences, and “Evil Flower,” a darker, more brooding track. Overall, it further cements Gallagher’s departure from the straightforward rock of his Oasis years. "Blue Moon Rising" (2020): This EP is arguably the most experimental of the three, leaning heavily into electronic and dance music. The title track, "Blue Moon Rising," is an atmospheric, pulsing song with driving beats and layered synths, reminiscent of 80s new wave. The EP also includes "Wandering Star," a more reflective and acoustic-driven song, and “Come On Outside,” which features a more traditional rock sound. With this EP, Gallagher showcases a mix of introspective songwriting and bold, new musical directions. Each of these EPs illustrates Noel Gallagher's ability to evolve his sound and explore different musical genres while maintaining his signature songwriting approach, blending his rock roots with elements of dance, electronic, and psychedelic music.
